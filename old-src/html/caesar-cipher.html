The Caesar Cipher

What is the Caesar Cipher?

The Caesar Cipher is a simplistic and well-known cipher attributed to Julius Caesar, who preferred employing a shift value of 3 (A would become D in encryption, D would become A in decryption). The cipher falls under the family of substition ciphers, where each letter in the "plaintext" is replaced by some letter a fix number of positions down the alphabet.

In this demonstration, any message that is desired to be encrypted is entered into the text area labeled "Plaintext." The act of encryption takes place upon pressing the "Encrypt" button. If decrypting a encoded message, enter the encoded text into the "Ciphertext" text area and press the "Decrypt" button. To clear all fields, press the "Reset" button. Enter a numerical value into the text area labeled "Shift" to change how many steps along the alphabet the text is encoded/decoded.
