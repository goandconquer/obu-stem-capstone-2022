The Substitution Cipher

What is the Substitution Cipher?

The Substitution Cipher is a more general implementation of the same fundamentals found within the Caesar Cipher demonstrated before, however it differs by employing a completely scrambled cipherkey alphabet instead of a static numeric value that shifts the standard alphabet. This form of cipher offers very little communication security, especially as messages become longer (more than several hundred ciphertext characters) as it can be easily broken by hand or with analytical tools that will be demonstrated. In some implementations, a ciper key is used to construct (or order) the substitution alphabet. However, in this demonstration, the working alphabet will already be determined.

Word Finder

This utility accepts a combination of letters and periods and attempts to find all possible matches that adheres to the pattern. Periods are placed in the position of unknown letters. For example, if you know that the word you are trying to find has a "b", an "r", and a "k" at the end, but do not know what the letters in the third and forth position are, you'll enter "br..k" into the text field and press submit. Then, upload the "dict.txt" file and wait for it to return the possible matches.
