The Rail Fence Cipher

What is the Rail Fence Cipher?

The Rail Fence Cipher (also known as the zig-zag cipher) falls under the family of transposition ciphers. The structure of the cipher is comprised of various "rails" or rows upon which successive letters from the plaintext are laid upon in a diagonal fashion (one element to the right, one element down) with the starting position being at the upper-left-most position in the structure. Once the plaintext has traversed the structure downward and encounters the last row, the plaintext's trajectory "bounces" and begins ascending diagonally along the rails (still to the right, but now upwards). It repeats this in fashion until the entire plain text has been mapped out this way.

The generation of the ciphertext occurs at this point, where the algorithm now reads right-to-left across the patterned plaintext until an encrypted string of letters is generated. In this cipher, the encrypted text is not made up of entirely new symbols or letters, but instead relies on the positional manipulation of the letters introduced in the plaintext.

In this demonstration, the text that is to be encrypted should be entered into the text area labeled "Plaintext." If there is text to be decrypted, it should be entered into the text area labeled "Ciphertext." The encryption of the plaintext takes place once the button labeled "Encrypt" has been pressed. The decryption of the ciphertext takes place once the button labeled "Decrypt" has been pressed. The fields will be reset to their default values once the button labeled "Reset" has been pressed. A visual representation of the cipher structure for the plaintext will be generated once the button labeled "Grid" has been placed. In order to set how many rails that the cipher structure should use, enter a numerical value into the text area labeled "Rail Key."
